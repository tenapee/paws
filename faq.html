Pet Adoption & Wellness Services

FAQs

  • Can I adopt a pet from PAWS?
  • While we are not involved in the actual adoption process, each pet's description includes the contact information for their adoption location. We serve merely as a resource to connect pet lovers with adoptable animals all over the country.
  • How old do I have to be to adopt?
  • Every adoption agency/shelter has different policies, but 21 is the typical age.
  • How do I apply to adopt a pet?
  • You can find all adoption information in the description of each adoptable pet. Most agencies/shelters require an application of some sort.
  • What is the difference between a shelter and rescue group?
  • The biggest difference is that most shelters keep their available pets in a single facility, while most rescue groups keep theirs in the homes of volunteers.
  • What should I look for when I go see the pet?
  • Just trust your intuition. If it seems right, chances are, it is. If something seems off, it probably is.
  • Are the pets I see still available for adoption?
  • We work very hardly to keep our data up-to-date. We use multiple resources to update our database every morning. If an animal is showing as available, it is.
  • How should I prepare for my new pet?
  • You should try your hardest to make it comfortable for your new pet. This is going to be a very different experience for both of you, so the more comfortable the better. Feel free to check out our resources for more information.
